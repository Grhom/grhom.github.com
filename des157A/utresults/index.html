User Test 1

Feedback Session

Vlad enjoyed the dice animation and the overall design interface. He commented on the animations, graphics, and colors. Some feedback he gave aligns with the questions I had in mind for my user testing.

1. What do you think can be improved to indicate who won in the game?
The winning indication is clear to Vlad because the sounds and graphics make it very intuitive. He enjoyed the bone visual and the puppy sounds that make it easy to show when someone wins.

2. What do you think can be improved to indicate the player’s turn?
He emphasized how the main issue with the game was improving the interface to help indicate the player’s turn. I think what confused him the most was the bug in my game. I noticed how the opposite player would receive points from the player that is rolling the dice. This error made it confusing for him to understand whose turn it was. In other instances, it worked according to the player’s role, but I will go back and fix this issue.

3. How intuitive was it for you to enter your name before you played the game?
He had some comments about the autocomplete and adding styling to the form field for the name to help match the theme I have.

User Test 2

Feedback Session

Ali enjoyed the styling and the overall theme of the interface. She wanted the options she could interact with, such as the slider for the sound, the help icon to refer back to the instructions, and the alert for the user testing. Similar to Vlad, Ali commented on some of the questions I prepared for. In addition, she commented on the buttons and how they are all styled the same. The start button isn’t functional after you start the game, so I plan to remove it and only have the restart button to make it more straightforward for the user.

1. What do you think can be improved to indicate who won in the game?
She commented on having words or a pop-up on the bottom of the screen when the roll button goes away to make it more straightforward for the user the game is over and a player has won. She mentioned that the game control area is the main focus and hot spot of the screen, making it more seamless to have an indicator in that screen section than the header.

2. What do you think can be improved to indicate the player’s turn?
Similarly, Ali commented to add pop-ups and text to clarify when the person’s turn has been switched.

3. How intuitive was it for you to enter your name before you played the game?
Ali suggested adding styling to the name section and setting up the form as an active stater.

User Test 3

Feedback Session

Cody also liked the interface and the designs of the game. The most important feedback I received that aligned with the other testers was the start button and the indication of the player’s turn. Cody also commented on more minor design details like padding and making the items on the page more centered aligned.

1. What do you think can be improved to indicate who won in the game?
He had no comments about the winning screen. He liked the visuals and thought the treat is a good indicator of who is receiving points and who wins the game after.

2. What do you think can be improved to indicate the player’s turn?
Similar to the other user tests, it was difficult to tell which player’s turn to roll the dice. Cody suggested making the border thicker and more prominent and making the other card more transparent.

3. How intuitive was it for you to enter your name before you played the game?
Cody commented how he wouldn’t know that he needed to enter names for the players if I didn’t add the user test screens.

Final implementation

From the feedback I received, I plan to adjust the game controls and clarify indicators. For the game controls, all of the users were unsure which buttons to click. Once the user clicks on the "start" button, it should disappear and only show the "roll" button. I also want to rearrange the heirachy of the buttons and but the main focus on the roll button.

For the indicators, there were confusions on the name fields and some confusions on who won the game. To make it more clear, I plan to seperate each step into separate screens than having many actions in one screen. After the user reads the instructions, one screen will appear with two scoreboards and they can only enter the player's name to continue. The next screen will be the game itseld

To make the scoreboards more clear, I plan to stylie brighter and boldder colors to highlight the player's turn and use pop-up text that explicitly indicates it.

Lastly, I will include an overlay of text on the bottom to indicate who won the game instead of leaving it on the header.
