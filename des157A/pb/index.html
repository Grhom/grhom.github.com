Project Summary

My project topic will be on “The Game of Dog(Pig)” and I am going to finish and update the game by adding the requirements needed and additional changes I plan to make.

I tried to find some websites that have similar objectives, goals, and interactions as my game. It was difficult to find a dog rolling dice game, so instead I added comparisons of different content and ideas that lead to the current game idea I have.

Comparison Project 1:

Website:Linked here

The first website is a rolling dice concept. I really enjoyed how interactive the dice is and the animations and sounds that mimic what a real life dice should look like. Other than the dice interaction, the page is very blank and there are too many ads and colors that are clustered and very distracting to me. The current Game of Pig website is very simple and has minimal interactions that can have more engagement for the user. I wanted to incorporate this animation into my rolling dice each time it was the player’s turn to make the interaction feel more lively.

Comparison Project 2:

Website: Linked here

The next content that inspired my game is the “Electric Tricky Vicious” dog game. The goal of the game is to grab as many bones before the dog will notice and will bite your hand. It is a game that I used to play when I was younger at school. I enjoy the concept of the game and wanted to incorporate it into the rolling dice game. In terms of the user interface design of the product, the dog looks very intimidating and does not match the aesthetic of what I plan to incorporate. The colors are loud and very bold, which can distract the user when they are trying to focus on rolling the dice. I didn’t follow the colors of the dog and incorporated colors and drawings that I was inspired from past corgi products I have used.

Project Goals:

The two projects I listed out are both very engaging and interactive with the user. The dice game realistically integrates sounds and animations to mimic someone rolling a dice. Similarly, the Electric Vicious dog game incorporates sounds and people to interact with objects that would trigger a dog in real life. They both have bold colors that are clashing and very distracting to play with. Instead, I plan to use softer colors and more bolded colors for CTA buttons and the dices to highlight the main points of the game. I plan to include sounds and animations that incorporate the dog and rolling dice interactions in my game to make it engaging and fun for the users to play with.

Design Strategies:

I will use bolder colors to highlight and emphasize important instructions, buttons, and objects for the users to pay attention to. Also, using information hierarchy will make it easy for users to read and understand what will be presented in the game. Incorporating interactive elements such as hover effects, overlays, pop-ups, and transitions will give good indications of what is happening in the game to the user. The typography will be simple, as it will not distract the main content from the game and effectively communicate to the user what is happening. Along with the simple typeface, the game will have a minimal design by having a balance of negative space around every object. Lastly, different states of buttons and animations will give even more indication and communication to the user.

Interaction Strategies:

Some interactions I plan to incorporate is a timer for the dices when they roll on each turn, animations when the dices are clicked to give the game more movements, sound effects for both the dog and the dices, and pop-ups to help indicate turns and the outcomes of the game. Implementing these visual and sound effects will give cues to the user and give a more pleasant experience! There is also an interactive form where users type their names in the scoreboard. Also, each time the user wins a point, a bone will appear in their bowl. If the dog gets angry and realizes you took their bone (each time you miss your turn, receive a one, or snake eyes) a bone will be taken away from the player’s bowl, but the score will remain the same.

Experience Goals:

The goal of the game is to try to receive as many points as they can as they roll their dice on their turns. It might also incentivize users to not take food from dogs, otherwise they might get upset! Overall, I want my players to enjoy the game and have fun with the other players they are competing with.
