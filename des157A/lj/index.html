Articulation of Thoughts

In the article, “20 best practices for form design,” the list structure of different points needed to optimize form design was very clear and easy to follow. Implementing these best practices and ideas helps improve the user experience. Some key takeaways that I learned from the article are column layouts, minimizing information, the order of information, and increasing accessibility. Minimizing input fields can help avoid unnecessary questions and only ask relevant information. Less information can help give a more pleasant experience for the user. Also, breaking down the information using the format, size inputs, the proximity of labels, and minimizing blocks of text can help reduce cognitive overload.

Feedback is also great for user experience, providing users a clear notice of whether they filled in inputs correctly or if their forms are submitted or need more changes, I find feedback forms helpful, as they instruct me what I need to do to complete the form. I also have an appreciation for minimal forms, as they are aesthetically pleasing and also give the most simple and direct experience for me.

Airbnb Form

The User Interface of Airbnb is a great example of forms: allowing people to book a place to stay and also creating trust between two strangers. During the initial search, the only fields you need to specify are choosing a destination users would like to go to, the duration of their stay, and the number of guests. The practice is friendly, simple, and streamlined.
